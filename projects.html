UC Irvine Chem-E Car Project

Role: Mechanical Team Member | June 2025 – Present

SolidWorks 3D Printing Mechanical Fabrication CAD Systems Integration

The Objective

Design and integrate the mechanical systems—including the chassis, powertrain, and battery enclosures—for a competition-ready chemically powered vehicle.

Design & Integration

Using SolidWorks, I modeled the vehicle's core structural components, ensuring precise spatial packaging for the chemical reaction housings. I coordinated heavily with the electrical subteam to optimize wiring layouts, PCB placement, and motor integration within the physical constraints of the chassis.

Manufacturing & Assembly

The final components were manufactured using a combination of 3D printing and mechanical fabrication to meet strict competition weight and safety constraints.

Engineering Challenge: Resolving the Drift

Previous iterations of the vehicle suffered from a persistent drift issue that negatively affected competition scoring. I analyzed the steering and alignment geometry, redesigning the component interfaces to ensure straight-line tracking. This mechanical overhaul significantly improved the vehicle's predictability and overall performance.

ASME PeterWorks — Drone Design & Build

Role: Student Engineer | Sept 2025 – Dec 2025

SolidWorks 3D Printing Laser Cutting Soldering Electronic Assembly UAV Calibration

The Objective

Participate in a comprehensive engineering program to design, manufacture, and assemble a fully functional quadcopter drone from scratch, bridging the gap between theoretical CAD and physical fabrication.

Design, Fabrication & Flight

I utilized SolidWorks to model custom frame components, optimizing for both weight and structural integrity. Through the program, I gained hands-on experience with 3D printing and laser cutting to produce the chassis, ensuring all mounting points for motors and flight controllers were precisely aligned.

The assembly phase involved precise soldering of the power distribution board and wiring of the flight electronics. I performed system calibrations and motor testing to ensure flight stability, culminating in a successful flight demonstration that validated the mechanical and electronic integration.
